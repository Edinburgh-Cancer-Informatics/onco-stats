## Basic Level Textbooks

These introductory texts focus on fundamental concepts and are highly recommended for those beginning their statistical training or preparing for exams.

| Title | Authors | Publisher | Key Highlights |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Medical Statistics: A Textbook for the Health Sciences (5th Ed)** | Stephen J. Walters, Michael J. Campbell, David Machin 

 | Wiley 

 | Uses NHS and UK clinical examples; includes multiple-choice exercises. Ideal for Scottish oncology trainees.

 |
| **An Introduction to Medical Statistics** | Martin Bland 

 | Oxford University Press 

 | A well-established text widely used in UK medical schools.

 |
| **Intuitive Biostatistics** | Harvey Motulsky 

 | Oxford University Press 

 | Focuses on conceptual understanding and intuition rather than mathematical formulas.

 |
| **Medical Statistics at a Glance** | Aviva Petrie and Caroline Sabin 

 | Wiley-Blackwell 

 | Visual, easy-to-digest format. Particularly useful for **FRCR exam preparation**.

 |

## Specialized Oncology & Research Methodology

Resources specifically tailored toward cancer research, clinical trials, and critical appraisal.

* 
**Clinical Trials in Cancer: Principles and Practice** * *Authors:* Girling, Parmar, Stenning, Stephens & Stewart 


* *Significance:* Definitive text on trial design, randomization, and endpoints. Essential for FRCR critical appraisal questions.




* 
**Epidemiology for the Uninitiated (5th Ed)** * *Authors:* Coggon, Barker & Rose 


* 
*Significance:* Concise guide to study design, bias, and confounding in cancer research.




* 
**Critical Appraisal of Epidemiological Studies and Clinical Trials (4th Ed)** * *Author:* Mark Elwood 


* 
*Significance:* Develops the analytical skills needed for evidence-based practice and interpreting study results.

## Online Resources & Guidelines

* 
**[CONSORT Statement](http://www.consort-statement.org):** Essential guidelines for reporting and appraising randomized controlled trials.


* 
**[Clinical Trials Tool Kit](http://www.ct-toolkit.ac.uk):** Comprehensive resource for sample size calculators and randomization tools.


* 
**[Statistical Notes in BMJ](http://www-users.york.ac.uk/~mb55/pubs/pbstnote.htm):** Martin Bland’s collection of short explanations regarding common statistical pitfalls.



---

## Intermediate Level Textbooks

These texts bridge basic concepts and advanced applications, with an emphasis on practical implementation in clinical trials.

* 
**Regression Modelling Strategies (2nd Ed)** - *Frank E. Harrell Jr.* Addresses real-world challenges like missing data and survival analysis using R.


* 
**Clinical Prediction Models: A Practical Approach** - *Ewout W. Steyerberg* Focuses on developing and validating prognostic models in oncology.


* 
**Fundamentals of Biostatistics** - *Bernard Rosner* Balances theory with practice, including 300+ medical research examples.



---

## Advanced Level Textbooks

For Masters' and PhD-level training, covering sophisticated methodologies for complex research.

* 
**Regression Modelling Strategies (Advanced Topics):** Covers Bayesian inference, model uncertainty, and repeated measures analysis.


* 
**Regression Models in Biostatistics:** Focuses on practical suggestions for real-world data challenges.
