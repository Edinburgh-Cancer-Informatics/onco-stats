## Question 2: Measures of Central Tendency

- B) The mean is always a better measure of centre than the median
- C) If a dataset has an even number of observations, the median is calculated as the mean of the two middle values
- D) The mode is the most frequently occurring value
- E) The median is less influenced by outliers than the mean

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answer</summary>

**Correct answers: A, C, D, E are TRUE**

**Explanation:**

- **A) TRUE** ‚Äì By definition, the median is the middle value when data are ordered from lowest to highest. 50% of values lie above and 50% below the median.

- **D) TRUE** ‚Äì The mode is defined as the most frequently occurring value in a dataset.

- **E) TRUE** ‚Äì The median is resistant to extreme values (outliers) because it only considers the position of values, not their magnitude. The mean incorporates all values in its calculation, making it sensitive to outliers.

- **D) TRUE** ‚Äì The range is simply calculated as (maximum value - minimum value).

- **E) TRUE** ‚Äì The standard deviation measures how far observations are from the mean on average. A larger SD indicates the data points are more spread out from the mean.
